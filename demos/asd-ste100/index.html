What is ASD-STE100

The standard for technical clarity

ASD-STE100, also known as Simplified Technical English (STE), is a controlled language guideline that regulates the wording and writing style used in technical publications and documentation.

Originally developed for the aerospace maintenance community, it has expanded across dozens of industries — anywhere that clear, unambiguous instructions matter for safety, compliance, and global reach.

"The most versatile and least proprietary form of Controlled English available."

— STE Industry Assessment, 2024
01 / Background
Originally created for aerospace
Developed in 1986 under the AECMA consortium for aircraft maintenance documentation. Boeing, Airbus, and Rolls-Royce mandate it for supplier technical documentation.
02 / Scope
Two sections: words and grammar
The specification defines an approved vocabulary of controlled words plus grammatical rules that together eliminate ambiguity and reduce cognitive load for readers worldwide.
03 / Accessibility
No specialist software required
Only basic English technical writing ability, the STE specification document, and practical training from experienced instructors. That's all you need to get started.
04 / Also known as
Simplified English (SE, STE)
Also referenced as AECMA Simplified English in historical documents. Current standard maintained by the AeroSpace and Defence Industries Association of Europe.

Writing Rules

STE in practice

See how STE transforms verbose, ambiguous technical prose into clear, safe instructions that anyone can understand.

Before STE Complex, ambiguous writing
Vocabulary / Rule W1
Utilise the fastening device to commence the attachment procedure.
3 non-approved words: utilise, fastening device, commence
Vocabulary / Rule W2
The technician must endeavour to ascertain the root cause.
"endeavour" and "ascertain" not in STE approved word list
Vocabulary / Rule W3
Perform a visual check to verify that the apparatus is functioning correctly.
"apparatus" is ambiguous; use the specific part name
Grammar / Rule G1 — Active Voice
The bolt should be tightened by the technician.
Passive voice hides who does the action
Grammar / Rule G2 — Verb Tense
When the valve has been opened, checking that pressure has stabilised is necessary.
Gerund structure is harder to process than imperative
Grammar / Rule G3 — Noun Clusters
Perform a fuel system pressure test valve inspection.
Four-noun string is ambiguous — which part is being inspected?
Sentences / Rule S1 — Length
Before starting the engine, ensure that all covers have been installed correctly, that all access panels are closed and latched, and that all ground crew are clear of the aircraft and have confirmed that they are in a safe position.
Procedural sentences must be ≤ 20 words
Sentences / Rule S2 — Referents
Remove the panel and the cover. Clean it thoroughly.
"it" is ambiguous — which part does the technician clean?
Procedures / Rule P1 — One Action
Remove the filter and clean it and reinstall it and torque to specification.
Multiple actions in one step create checklist errors
Procedures / Rule P2 — Warnings
The engine oil is hot and can cause burns, so be careful when you drain it.
Safety warnings must precede the action, not follow it
After STE Clear, approved STE writing
Vocabulary / Rule W1
Use the bolt to start the attachment procedure.
Approved words: use, bolt, start — precise and unambiguous
Vocabulary / Rule W2
The technician must try to find the root cause.
"try" and "find" are approved STE words
Vocabulary / Rule W3
Look at the pump to make sure it operates correctly.
Specific part name removes all ambiguity
Grammar / Rule G1 — Active Voice
Tighten the bolt.
Active imperative: subject is clearly the reader/technician
Grammar / Rule G2 — Verb Tense
Open the valve. Make sure the pressure is stable.
Two imperative steps — sequential, unambiguous
Grammar / Rule G3 — Noun Clusters
Inspect the test valve of the fuel system pressure circuit.
Prepositional phrases clarify the noun relationships
Sentences / Rule S1 — Length
1. Install all covers correctly.
2. Close and latch all access panels.
3. Make sure all ground crew are clear of the aircraft.
4. Start the engine.
Four steps of ≤ 10 words each — safe and clear
Sentences / Rule S2 — Referents
Remove the panel and the cover. Clean the cover thoroughly.
Explicit referent eliminates ambiguity completely
Procedures / Rule P1 — One Action
1. Remove the filter.
2. Clean the filter.
3. Install the filter.
4. Torque the filter to the specified value.
One action per step — safe for checklist compliance
Procedures / Rule P2 — Warnings
WARNING: The engine oil is hot. It can cause burns.

Drain the engine oil.
Warning precedes the hazardous action — as required
Why STE

The measurable difference

01
Reduce translation costs by up to 40%
Shorter, simpler sentences with controlled vocabulary translate faster and with fewer errors, directly reducing per-word costs and review cycles.
02
Improve global documentation readability
Non-native English readers, machine translation systems, and voice assistants all process STE-compliant text more accurately than standard prose.
03
Achieve regulatory compliance
For suppliers in aerospace, defence, and medical sectors, STE compliance is often a contractual requirement with Boeing, Airbus, and Rolls-Royce.
04
Reduce maintenance errors
Unambiguous instructions mean technicians spend less time interpreting and more time executing — reducing rework and improving safety outcomes.
05
Works for writers of all levels
STE is a learnable framework, not an innate talent. Teams with mixed writing experience all converge on the same consistent output after training.
